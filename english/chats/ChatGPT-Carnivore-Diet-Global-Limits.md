## 1️⃣ Meat required

For 150 people:

``` math

150 \text{ kg/day}
```

Per year:

``` math

150 \times 365 = 54,750 \text{ kg/year}
```

Applying a **safety factor of 2**:

``` math

54,750 \times 2 = 109,500 \text{ kg/year}
```

So the village needs **109,500 kg of beef per year**.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 2️⃣ How much meat does one cow provide?

A typical beef cow:

- Live weight: ~600 kg
- Carcass weight: ~380 kg
- Edible boneless meat: ~230–270 kg

We’ll use a realistic average:

``` math

\approx 250 \text{ kg edible meat per cow}
```

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 3️⃣ Cows slaughtered per year

``` math

109,500 \div 250 = 438 \text{ cows per year}
```

So the village must slaughter **about 438 cows per year**.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 4️⃣ Total herd size (sustainable system)

You cannot slaughter the entire herd each year — cows must reproduce.

In a sustainable cattle system, roughly **15–25% of the herd** can be harvested annually.  
Let’s assume **20% annual offtake** (a reasonable figure).

``` math

438 \div 0.20 = 2,190 \text{ total cattle}
```

- We assumed **moderate pasture productivity** (1.5 ha per cow).

### ❗Trade-offs

- Using **all arable land for pasture** means no plant food production.
