## Where the Idea Comes From (Pseudoscience)

People historically tried to link skull/face shape with character or intelligence:

- **Phrenology** (18th–19th century): claimed that bumps and slopes on the skull indicate mental traits.
- **Physiognomy**: suggested personality could be read from facial features.

These frameworks are considered invalid and biased today.

## Why These Stereotypes Aren’t Reliable

Human faces vary widely for many biological reasons unrelated to personality:

- Skull shape is influenced by genetics and growth patterns
- Facial features don’t encode psychological traits
- Personality is shaped by environment, experience, and complex brain biology

## If You’re Interested in Personality Research…

There *are* well-validated frameworks that relate traits to behavior, such as:

- **Big Five Personality Traits** (Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, Neuroticism)
- **Myers-Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI)** (popular in some circles, but less scientifically robust than the Big Five)

None of these are based on physical features.
