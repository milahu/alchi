## Prompt:

how do we call the interdisciplinary science which is exactly between psychology (the study of human individuals) and sociology (the study of human relations)?  
i mean "psychology" specifically in the sense of "personality psychology"

## Response:

You’re circling a space that *a lot* of fields crowd into, so there isn’t one single universally agreed-upon name—but there **are a few well-established labels**, each with a slightly different emphasis.

Here are the closest and most commonly accepted ones, from most to least “exactly in between”:

### **Social Psychology** (the canonical answer)

This is the standard interdisciplinary field **bridging personality psychology and sociology**.

- Focus: how **individual personalities, traits, and cognitive processes** interact with **social structures, groups, norms, and relationships**
- Core questions:
  - How do personality traits express themselves in social contexts?
  - How do relationships, groups, and norms shape individual behavior?
- Historically, it literally grew *between* psychology and sociology and still sits there institutionally.

If you mean *personality psychology in interaction*, this is usually what people expect.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### **Psychological Sociology** (less common, more sociological)

This flips the center of gravity slightly toward sociology.

- Focus: how **social structures and roles** are internalized into the self
- Draws heavily on:
  - Symbolic interactionism
  - Mead, Cooley (“looking-glass self”)
- Used mostly inside sociology departments

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### **Personality and Social Psychology**

Not a separate discipline, but a **recognized subfield** (and journal category).

- Explicitly combines:
  - personality traits
  - interpersonal dynamics
  - social situations
- If you’re being precise in academic contexts, this label is often ideal.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### **Interpersonal Psychology** (narrower)

- Focuses specifically on **dyadic and relational processes**
- Less about society at large, more about face-to-face relationships

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### **Social Neuroscience** (only if biology is involved)

- Bridges personality, social behavior, and brain processes
- Not purely between psych and sociology, but adjacent

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### So, if I had to pin it down:

- **Most accurate and standard:** **Social Psychology**
- **Most explicit about personality:** **Personality and Social Psychology**
- **Most sociologically flavored:** **Psychological Sociology**
