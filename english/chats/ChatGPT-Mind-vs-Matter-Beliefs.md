## Response:

You’re mixing several different axes together and treating them like a single opposition. That’s why some of your pairings feel intuitive (“mind vs matter”) while others don’t really line up (e.g., “left” vs “hard power,” or “constructivism” vs “manifesting”). It’s not one clean divide—there are at least **three separate philosophical dimensions** tangled together:

- **Metaphysics** (what is fundamentally real: mind or matter?)
- **Epistemology** (how we know things: constructed vs discovered)
- **Ethics / politics / leadership** (values, power, organization)

If you separate those, the labels become much clearer—and less contradictory.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

1)  Metaphysics: “mind-first” vs “matter-first”

------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Mind-first camp (consciousness is primary):**

- Idealism (classic umbrella term)
- Panpsychism (consciousness everywhere)
- Dualism (mind distinct from matter)
- Phenomenology (focus on experience)
- Subjectivists
- Mentalists (though informal)

**Matter-first camp (reality is independent of mind):**

- Physicalism
- Materialism
- Realism (mind-independent world)
- Naturalism

These are the cleanest, most standard labels for your “core divide.”

------------------------------------------------------------------------

2)  Epistemology: “constructed reality” vs “discovered reality”

------------------------------------------------------------------------

This is where “constructivist” fits—but it’s not the same as idealism.

**Reality as constructed/interpreted:**

- Constructivism
- Postmodernism
- Relativism
- Interpretivists

**Reality as objective/discoverable:**

- Objectivism
- Empiricism
- Positivists
- Rationalists (partly overlapping)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

3)  Ethics & motivation: intentions vs outcomes

------------------------------------------------------------------------

This is a completely different axis.

**Intentions / morality-first:**

- Deontology
- Moralists
- Idealists (in everyday language, not philosophical idealism)
- Virtue ethicists

**Outcomes / consequences-first:**

- Consequentialism
- Utilitarianism
- Pragmatists

------------------------------------------------------------------------

4)  Power & leadership styles

------------------------------------------------------------------------

Again, separate dimension:

**“Soft / belief-driven / symbolic” framing:**

- Soft Power
- Transformational leadership
- Vision-driven leadership
- Charismatic leadership

**“Hard / material / incentive-based” framing:**

- Hard Power
- Transactional leadership
- Realpolitik
- Mechanistic management

------------------------------------------------------------------------

5)  Cultural / informal labels (less precise, more loaded)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

These are closer to what you were listing, but they’re **not neutral categories**:

**Mind-first / belief-heavy (informal or critical tone):**

- “New Age”
- Spiritualists
- Mystics
- Idealists (casual usage)
- “Manifestation” culture
- Esoteric thinkers

**Matter-first / skeptical:**

- Skeptics
- Reductionists
- Scientistic (sometimes used critically)
- Hard-nosed realists
- Determinists

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Where your grouping breaks down

A few examples of mismatches in your list:

- “Constructivist” ≠ “idealism” (one is about knowledge, the other about reality)
- “Left” vs “hard power” is political, not metaphysical
- “Nihilism” is about meaning, not matter vs mind
- “Weak leaders” vs “strong leaders” is a value judgment, not a philosophy

So instead of one binary, you’ve built a **compressed worldview** that merges:

> ontology + knowledge theory + ethics + politics + personality judgments

That compression *feels* powerful, but it hides important distinctions.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## If you want clean opposing labels

The most defensible pairings are:

- **Idealism vs Materialism**
- **Constructivism vs Objectivism**
- **Deontology vs Consequentialism**
- **Soft power vs Hard power**

Those are widely used and won’t confuse people.
