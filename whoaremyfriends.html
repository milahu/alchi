The Strange Situation

How do we find the basetype? The basetype can be tested with one-year-old children. In psychology, this is known as The Strange Situation.

  1. A child and its mother visit an unknown place.
  2. We let the child play for some time.
  3. An unknown mother joins the scene.
  4. We let the child play for some time.
  5. The mother quickly leaves the child, without saying bye.
  6. When does the child start crying? Now or later?
  7. We let the child cry for some time.
  8. The mother returns.
  9. When does the child stop crying? Now or later?

Here we see two decisions: start crying and stop crying. For each decision, we have two choices: now or later. The two decisions represent the two-factor model of personality.

  • 13 = broad sense = start crying now
  • 24 = long sense = start crying later. These children inhibit their own crying, and when their inhibition breaks, they start crying.
  • 14 = long move = stop crying now
  • 23 = broad move = stop crying later. These children are so "involved" in their crying, that they will act angry (3) or avoidant (2) when the mother returns.

The Strange Situation is part of Attachment Theory. The authors of Attachment Theory believe that the children's behavior is learned (nurture over nature, mind over matter), and they believe that type 1 (secure attachment = balanced attachment) is the ideal type, and all other types should be made (educated) more like type 1.
