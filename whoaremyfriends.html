Four Basetypes

In my compatibility chart, personality type is encoded with the four numbers 1234. Translation to Carl Jung: 1234 = Ntuiting Sensing Feeling Thinking.

These four basetypes are the result of my two-factor model of personality. I call these two factors "sense" and "move". Sense encodes the natural bias of the sensory nervous systems. Move encodes the natural bias of the motory nervous systems.

Four Properties

Every basetype is the combination of two properties. Every property can be described as the "common ground" of two basetypes. Translations to Hans Eysenck

  • 13 = broad sense = extravert
  • 24 = long sense = introvert
  • 23 = broad move = neurotic
  • 14 = long move = psychotic

13, 24, 23, 14: These are pairs of two basetypes. One and Three, Two and Four, Two and Three, One and Four. Short: One-Three, Two-Four, Two-Three, One-Four.

These are not just mental properties, but they also influence the body shape. sense = upper body, move = lower body.

This means

  • 13 = broad sense = broad shoulders = young brain
  • 24 = long sense = narrow shoulders = old brain
  • 23 = broad move = broad hips = feminine brain
  • 14 = narrow move = narrow hips = masculine brain

Subtypes

Every basetype has three subtypes

  • extraverted
  • ambiverted
  • introverted

The combination of basetype and subtype is called mixtype. Every mixtype can also be described as a mix of two basetypes. For example, here are the mixtypes of basetype 1

  • 1 extraverted = 1x3
  • 1 ambiverted = 1x1
  • 1 introverted = 1x4

In total, we have twelve mixtypes. This is the reason why we have twelve hours on the clock, and twelve months in the year.
