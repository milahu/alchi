Iodine deficiency affects roughly two billion people worldwide.

Iodine is required for every thyroid hormone your body makes.

Your thyroid regulates your metabolism, your temperature, your heart rate, your cognitive function, and your development from the moment of conception.

Soil erosion has stripped iodine from agricultural land.

Fluoride, bromide, and chlorine: all in your water, your bread, your swimming pools, occupy the same receptor sites as iodine and compete for absorption.

We have an epidemic of thyroid dysfunction.

We prescribe levothyroxine.

We do not discuss the tap water.

Iodine is the supplement I actually take. Not because it's exciting. Because it's the one thing that's quietly, systematically missing.
