Wool manages moisture, regulates temperature, resists fire, and biodegrades at end of life.

The polyester that replaced it sheds microplastics into the ocean with every wash. Those microplastics are now in fish tissue, in drinking water, and in human blood.

Beeswax candles don't release carcinogens.
Paraffin ones do.

Tallow is the most stable cooking fat at high temperature.
Seed oils produce toxic aldehydes when heated.

Bone broth provides glycine, collagen, and joint support compounds.
The powdered supplement attempting to replicate it costs £35 and is less bioavailable.

Fermented dairy provides K2, beneficial bacteria, and fat-soluble vitamins.
The probiotic supplement attempting to replicate it costs £20 a month and lives in a capsule.

In every case, without exception, the thing biology made first works better than the thing chemistry made to replace it.

This is not nostalgia.

This is a pattern that should make you furious.
