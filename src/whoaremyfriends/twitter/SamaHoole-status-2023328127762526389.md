Fat was always the prize. Not protein. Not plants. Fat.

Traditional hunters didn't just kill animals: they selected for the fattiest animals at the fattiest time of year.

Spring hunt: Target pregnant females (fat reserves for pregnancy)

Autumn hunt: Target animals fattened for winter

Winter hunt: Focus on species with thickest fat stores

Lean meat without fat? Often fed to dogs or left behind. Humans wanted the organs, bone marrow, and fat.

The term "rabbit starvation" exists because lean meat without fat can kill you. Early explorers who ate only lean game died despite abundant calories. They needed fat.

Your brain is 60% fat. Your hormones are made from cholesterol. Your cells are wrapped in fatty membranes. Every signaling molecule in your body depends on fat.

We didn't evolve to eat lean chicken breast with vegetables. We evolved to eat the fattiest cuts of the fattiest animals we could kill.

Everything about human biology screams: GIVE ME FAT.

And for 2.5 million years, we obliged.
