England, 1363. Sumptuary Laws enacted.

What the nobility could eat:
- Beef, venison, mutton, pork
- Goose, duck, pheasant
- Multiple meat courses per meal
- Rich gravies and sauces
- Cheese, butter, cream

What peasants were restricted to:
- Bread (barley, rye, occasionally wheat)
- Pottage (grain and vegetable stew)
- Occasional scraps of meat on feast days
- Ale (because water was unsafe)

This wasn't about nutrition. This was about maintaining class hierarchy through food access.

The wealthy stayed healthy, strong, and tall on animal products.

The peasants were shorter, weaker, and disease-prone on grain-based diets.

Fast forward 700 years:

Modern dietary guidelines recommend the peasant diet.
Call the aristocrat diet "unsustainable" and "elitist."

The irony is perfect.
