Here is how sugar addiction begins.

You are born. The formula has sugar in it. The baby food has sugar in it. The snacks designed specifically for toddlers have sugar in them. The dopamine pathway is being trained before you can speak.

You fall over and hurt yourself. You are given something sweet. The pathway records: sugar resolves distress.

You do well at school. You are given something sweet. The pathway records: sugar is reward.

It's your birthday. The table is covered in it. Everyone is happy. The pathway records: sugar is celebration, sugar is love, sugar is the sensation of being gathered with people who are glad you exist.

You're bored on a Sunday afternoon. You're given something sweet. The pathway records: sugar resolves boredom.

You're anxious about something. You're given something sweet. The pathway records: sugar is comfort, sugar is safety, sugar is how adults show care.

By the time you are seven years old, sugar has been mapped onto every significant emotional state you've experienced. It is not just a food preference. It is the emotional regulation system. It is the language of comfort, reward, celebration, and care.

And then you're an adult, and you're stressed, and you open the fridge, and you don't fully know why.

This didn't start last week.

It started in the first year of your life, deliberately, by people who understood exactly what they were doing.
