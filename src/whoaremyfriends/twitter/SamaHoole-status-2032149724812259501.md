The nightshade family: tomatoes, potatoes, peppers, aubergine, goji berries, produce glycoalkaloids as a defence compound. In potatoes, the primary one is solanine. In tomatoes, it is tomatine.

Solanine inhibits an enzyme called acetylcholinesterase, which breaks down the neurotransmitter acetylcholine. Too much acetylcholine at nerve junctions: overstimulation, muscle weakness, gut cramping, in sufficient quantities, which green potatoes can provide: nausea, vomiting, neurological symptoms.

Green potatoes and sprouted potatoes contain enough solanine to cause real symptoms. This is why you are told not to eat green potatoes. The green is the solanine producing chlorophyll: the plant signalling that it is activating its defence chemistry.

The ripe, stored, ungreened potato has much lower levels: low enough to be tolerable for most people in normal quantities.

The person with autoimmune conditions, joint pain, or gut issues who improves dramatically when nightshades are removed is not experiencing a placebo effect. They are removing a class of compounds that was already causing low-grade inflammation in a system that was already struggling.

This is documented in rheumatology literature.
