Linoleic acid, the primary fat in seed oils, has a half-life of approximately 600 days in human adipose tissue.

That means the seed oil you ate in 2024 is still in your body.

It incorporates into cell membranes. It oxidises under heat, light, and metabolic stress. It produces 4-HNE and other aldehydes that damage mitochondria and trigger inflammation.

The standard diet provides roughly 20 to 30 grams of linoleic acid per day.

The diet humans evolved on provided roughly 2 to 4 grams per day.

We increased it by a factor of ten in about fifty years.

The cell membranes have not issued a formal statement.

The inflammatory disease statistics have.
