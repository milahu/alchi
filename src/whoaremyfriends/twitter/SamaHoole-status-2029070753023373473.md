Sheep milk is more nutritious than cow milk by almost every measure.

Higher in protein. Higher in fat. Higher in calcium: nearly double cow milk per serving.

Higher in B12. Higher in conjugated linoleic acid. Richer in fat-soluble vitamins.

The fat globules are smaller and more digestible than cow milk, for similar reasons to goat milk.

You almost never see it in British supermarkets.

You see it processed into cheese: Manchego, Pecorino, Roquefort, Feta, Ricotta in its traditional form. These are among the oldest cheeses in the world, produced from a milk that has been valued for its richness since antiquity.

Sheep were milked in Britain until the medieval period, when cow dairying expanded and largely displaced it.

The more nutritious milk became the artisan cheese at the farmers' market.

The less nutritious one became the skimmed variety in the semi-skimmed carton.

We made several interesting choices along the way.
