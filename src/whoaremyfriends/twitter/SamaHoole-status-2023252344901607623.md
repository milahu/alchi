For 10,000 years, humans drink raw milk fresh from animals. It's the original superfood: complete protein, bioavailable calcium, fat-soluble vitamins, beneficial bacteria. Nomadic cultures survive entirely on raw milk and meat. The Maasai drink three litres daily and have exceptional bone density and cardiovascular health.

1864: Louis Pasteur develops pasteurisation for wine and beer preservation. It's meant for alcohol, not milk. The process involves heating to 72°C for 15 seconds. This kills bacteria but also denatures proteins, destroys enzymes, and reduces vitamin content.

1890s: Urban dairies operate in terrible conditions. Cows fed distillery waste, confined in filthy spaces, milked by diseased workers. This "swill milk" makes people sick. The problem isn't raw milk: it's industrial dairy operations with no hygiene standards.

The solution should be: Clean farms, healthy cows, sanitary handling. Instead, they mandate pasteurisation. This allows dairies to maintain terrible conditions because heat processing kills the bacteria that would otherwise poison customers. The treatment addresses symptoms, not causes.

1924: Pasteurisation becomes widely mandatory across American cities. Raw milk is increasingly restricted. The narrative shifts: Raw milk is dangerous, pasteurised milk is safe. This is presented as settled science despite humans thriving on raw milk for millennia.

The effects are immediate: Lactose intolerance increases. People who could digest raw milk fine cannot digest pasteurised milk. The process destroys lactase enzymes that help digest milk sugar. Native bacteria that aid digestion are killed. The food becomes harder to process.

Modern raw milk regulations are absurd: In many jurisdictions, you can buy cigarettes and alcohol freely but raw milk is criminalized. Farmers selling raw milk face raids, fines, imprisonment. The enforcement is aggressive. Meanwhile, pasteurised milk consumption declines because people correctly intuit it's nutritionally inferior.

The establishment claims raw milk causes disease. The data shows otherwise: Between 1998-2011, CDC attributed 2 deaths to raw milk. In the same period, over 3,000 deaths were linked to pasteurised dairy products. The banned product is statistically safer.

We criminalised the traditional food and mandated the processed substitute. Your grandmother drank raw milk daily and never questioned it. You need a criminal enterprise to access what humans considered normal nutrition for 10,000 years.
