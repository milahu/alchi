In the Second World War, when rationing hit Britain, meat was one of the first things restricted.

Not because it was unhealthy.

Because it was *valuable*.

The government understood, without needing a dietary study, that meat was the nutritional priority. So it went first to the military, then to heavy industrial workers, then to the general population on a points system.

Children and pregnant women got priority allocations.

Not for the bread. For the meat and the full-fat dairy.

The people running the country in an existential crisis fed the most vulnerable members of society animal protein.

Decades later their successors tell us it was unnecessary.
