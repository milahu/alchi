Three meals a day.

Right. Yes. Breakfast, lunch, dinner. The eternal rhythm. The ancient wisdom. The foundation of human health since time immemorial.

Since time immemorial being, specifically, the nineteenth century. When factory owners needed a framework for worker feeding breaks that didn't disrupt the shift pattern. When the Industrial Revolution required humans to eat on a schedule that suited the machinery rather than their own physiology. When breakfast became a meal because someone needed to sell grain products to people who were now, inconveniently, awake and near a factory at 7am.

Before that: you ate when there was food. You didn't eat when there wasn't. This was not a hardship. This was Tuesday.

Your Palaeolithic ancestors did not pack a lunch. They did not schedule a mid-afternoon snack. They did not set a reminder to have their pre-workout protein at 4pm and their dinner at 6:30pm and their casein shake before bed.

They hunted. They found something. They ate until they couldn't. They slept. They got up and did it again, whenever the food presented itself, which was not three times a day on a reliable schedule with a Waitrose delivery backup.

The three meals a day schedule is a Victorian industrial management tool.

You have been eating around it your entire life as though it were biology.

It isn't.
